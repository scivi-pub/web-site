    Scivi Platform

    Scivi (Latin: I knew; I understood) is a nonprofit, open-source project that aims to radically improve Academic publishing. We publish, and reward with Scivi token, the peer-reviewed papers in all major areas of science and math, focusing on science "dark matter": mini-reviews (that are often included in e.g. grant applications); single reproducible results (statistically sound results of a single set of experiments); and the replication studies, along with regular reports and articles

    Scivi Token

    Scivi is a "utility" token that uses Ethereum blockchain to pay contributing authors, referees and editors

    Scivi is an ERC20 token, which emission is governed by "Proof-of-Publication" and "Proof-of-Citation" mechanisms. The smart contract allows us to "mint" new Scivi tokens (3 000 000 max) only once a quarter to pay for contributions, to reward already published papers and the scientists according to their citation record

    The unused tokens will be burned

    During the summer 2018 we plan to integrate payments using Stellar and EOS networks and later we plan to develop our own blockchain for payments and for tracking the publication process - from the submission to post-publication peer-review. In 2019 we plan to list Scivi on exchanges and in 2020 will start taking fees for the publications in our journals in Scivi tokens
